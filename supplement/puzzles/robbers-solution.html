Interrogation

The police rounded up Jim, Bud and Sam yesterday, because one of them was suspected of having robbed a bank. The three suspects made the following statements under intensive questioning:

  • Jim: I'm innocent.
  • Bud: I'm innocent.
  • Sam: Bud is guilty.

If exactly one of these statements is true, prove who robbed the bank. (Your proof must be irrefutable; it may not rely on your feeling that one testimony is “suspicious.”) Write your solution and proof, save it as PDF, and upload it to Dropbox on eCourseware.

Solution

Three statements
Jim: I'm innocent.
Bud: I'm innocent.
Sam: Bud is guilty.
Three options
Jim: TRUE
Bud: FALSE
Sam: FALSE
Jim: FALSE
Bud: TRUE
Sam: FALSE
Jim: FALSE
Bud: FALSE
Sam: TRUE

Because Bud's and Sam's statements cannot both be false, Jim's statement cannot be true. Therefore, Jim's statement is false. The other options are irrelevant. (My wife found a different method to solve this problem. Can you guess?)
