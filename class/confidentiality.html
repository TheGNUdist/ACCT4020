Encryption

  1. Select a (short) phrase to encrypt and write it down without spaces or punctuation.
  2. Select a cipher word that is shorter than the phrase to encrypt.
  3. Write the cipher word letter-for-letter under your phrase. Repeat the cipher word until the end of the phrase. Truncate the cipher word at the end, if necessary.

For example, if the phrase were “accounting architecture” and the cipher word were “coyne,” then under accountingarchitecture (no spaces), I would write coynecoynecoynecoyneco.

Vigenère Cipher

  1. On the Vigenère Square, find the column that is the first letter in the phrase and the row that is the first letter in the cipher word. The intersection is the first letter of the encrypted message. Repeat.

For example, for accountingarchitecture and coyne, the intersection of a and c is c; the intersection of c and o is q; the intersection of c and y is a, and so on. The first three letters of the encrypted message are cqa.

Vigenère Cipher

  1. Reverse these steps to decrypt an encrypted message.

For example, for an encrypted message with the first three letters of cqa and a cipher word of coyne, c in row c is in column a; q in row o is in column c; a in row y is in column c. The first three letters of the decryped message are acc.
