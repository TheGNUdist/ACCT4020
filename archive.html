Open

This lecture introduces several forms of open content. Understanding open technologies is an important precursor to understanding enterprise-grade information systems. This is also the first topic for which we will have student presentations. The final lecture contains a list of extra-credit topics.

For the online students: Five presentations for this topic are due Wednesday, February 3 @ 23.59. You have until end of business on Friday to email me to volunteer for a presentation. Also, I will add a quiz to eCourseware for evaluating these presentations. Every student, except for the presenters, should complete this evaluation by Friday, February 5 @ 23.59. Once the presentations have been posted to eCourseware, I will ask each presenter one or more questions, the presenter will have until Thursday, February 4 @ 23.59 to respond. This will be the model for all subsequent presentation dates, as well. Please review the syllabus for grading and submission guidelines.

January 26, 2016

Accounting Architecture

Accounting Architecture is a model of the components of an accounting information system. The Accounting Architecture framework is the foundation for this course, and this lecture introduces the framework and the topics we will discuss for the remainder of the semester.

Lecture Readings
January 21, 2016

Professionalism

Before we get into the technical content for this course, I would like first to talk about a your future as a professional. I hope that this lecture will inspire you as you prepare yourself for job interviews, internships, and careers.

Some of the readings for this topic are from the University of Memphis library and require a login to access. Remember, the readings are for the live and the online sections. Also, the final lecture slide contains an assignment that will count towards your Participation grade. The online students should submit their memos to Dropbox by Tuesday, January 26 @ 23.59. The live students should bring their memos to class on Tuesday, January 26.

One reason for holding this lecture at the beginning of the semester is to prepare students for Beta Alpha Psi’s Meet the Firms event. This event will be Friday, January 29 from 17.30 to 19.00 on the first floor of the Fogelman Classroom Building. This is an opportunity to network with your future employers. Please dress professionally and bring copies of your resume.
